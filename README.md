### Hi, I'm Peter

I spend a lot of time asking *why* things happen, not just *what* happened. Over time, that curiosity naturally expanded into cybersecurity, where understanding how systems behave is essential for detecting, investigating, and responding to security events.

I hold the CompTIA Security+ certification and have completed the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate, along with a range of practical labs and learning paths focused on security operations, digital forensics, incident response, detection engineering, and cloud security fundamentals. I prefer learning by doing, so most of the work here reflects hands-on investigations, operational workflows, and technical exercises rather than passive study.

This repository serves as a personal workspace where I document investigations, operational workflows, detection ideas, automation projects, and technical research that help me build stronger analytical habits used throughout modern security operations.

---

### How I Approach Security Work

I prefer learning through direct analysis rather than simply following walkthroughs.

Most of the work documented here reflects practical exercises designed to help me understand:

* how system activity is recorded in logs, telemetry, and forensic artifacts
* how suspicious behavior differs from normal operational activity
* how multiple weak signals can correlate into meaningful findings
* how analysts validate conclusions and communicate results
* how detection logic can be developed from observed behavior

Because of my background in technical documentation, I place a strong emphasis on:

* clearly documenting investigation steps
* explaining reasoning and decision points
* validating findings through evidence
* structuring information for operational handoff
* creating repeatable and understandable workflows

The goal is not just to reach an answer, but to clearly demonstrate how the answer was reached.
